# Deterministic research baselines

Phase 5 checkpoints 3A and 3B form a closed schedule-generation and source-attestation layer
between curated evidence and the existing scenario assembler. They create deterministic research
controls; they are not an execution system, a generic strategy language, a ranking engine, or
approval for live trading.

## Closed catalog and specification

Only schema/version 1 of these policies exists:

| Baseline | Supplied quantity | Market evidence | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `flat_control` | forbidden | none | explicit no-exposure control |
| `static_long` | positive absolute quantity | none | positive directional exposure control |
| `static_short` | positive absolute quantity | none | negative directional exposure control |
| `lagged_funding_receiver` | positive absolute quantity | actual hourly funding | receive the sign of the settled rate only after it becomes known |

The strict JSON contract rejects unknown or duplicate fields and binary floats. It requires schema
and policy versions, venue, instrument, UTC start-inclusive/end-exclusive boundaries, a native decision
interval, and positive Decimal-string initial cash. Non-flat baselines require a positive finite
Decimal-string absolute target. The funding policy additionally requires a 3,600-second interval,
one-second grid tolerance, and `missing_data_policy: "fail"`. Optional bounded researcher labels
and notes are descriptive and excluded from economic analytical identity. The contract contains no
leverage, margin, liquidation, portfolio, price, or fill parameter.

## Temporal rules and sign convention

Schedules begin with an economic position of zero. Static controls issue their sole target at the
study start. The funding receiver considers only actual, non-predicted Hyperliquid funding events
with successful same-venue ingestion lineage. Expected logical settlement slots are hourly UTC
boundaries. An original event timestamp may differ from its logical slot by at most one second and
is never rewritten. The logical slot exists only for expected coverage, duplicate/conflict
detection, and native-grid validation; it is not an information time or a replacement timestamp.

At settlement `t`, funding is applied to the position held immediately before `t`. Only then does
the observed rate become eligible as a signal. Positive funding means longs pay and shorts receive,
so the next target is short. Negative funding makes the next target long. Exact zero makes it flat.
Policy v1 defines information availability as the exact exchange event timestamp. The generated
target time is separately projected to the first declared native decision boundary greater than or
equal to that availability time; it is never rounded backward. This projection is explicit: with a
`1h` decision interval, `00:00:00.500Z` becomes a `01:00:00Z` target decision, while a finer
declared interval produces its corresponding earlier boundary. The logical hourly slot does not
cause that delay; the researcher-supplied decision interval does.

The assembler then applies its separate execution latency and selects the first eligible modeled
candle-open fill. Same-time accounting ordering remains funding, fill, mark, so the position
generated by a settlement cannot receive that settlement. With zero latency, an exactly on-grid
event may be eligible for an equal-timestamp candle-open fill after funding. That equality is a
deterministic simulation convention: the open is a modeling proxy, not evidence that a
post-settlement price was executable. Positive latency or stricter assumptions should be used when
same-time execution is unrealistic. Repeated unchanged targets are suppressed; a sign reversal is
one target change.

No pre-window observation seeds the policy, including a timestamp that is within tolerance before
the first logical slot. An event exactly at the start is eligible at the start but its funding still
precedes the resulting fill. The end boundary is excluded. Funding baseline boundaries must be
exact UTC hours; partial first or last funding intervals are invalid requests rather than silently
partial coverage.

## Fail-closed evidence and provenance

Generation stops with `needs_data` when any expected hourly slot is missing or duplicated, or when
evidence is conflicting, predicted, non-hourly, outside the one-second grid tolerance, for another
instrument, or lacks successful ingestion provenance. There is no imputation, forward fill,
silent skip, stale substitution, or price-based fallback. Candles, mark, index, mid, oracle,
reports, and evaluation results are not signal inputs.

Each portable funding observation preserves exchange event time, exact Decimal rate, normalized
venue/instrument, interval, actual/predicted classification, stable ingestion source labels, and a
canonical row-evidence SHA-256. Decision dispositions separately preserve the exchange event time,
logical funding slot, information-availability time, projected target-decision time, evidence hash,
rule, target, and policy/version. SQLite IDs, local paths, receipt/ingestion clocks, and generation
clocks are excluded. The source identity hashes sorted portable evidence; the analytical identity
hashes the economic specification, source identity, and generated target sequence. Researcher
prose does not change it.

## Artifact authority and compatibility

The closed bundle contains exactly:

- `baseline-spec.json`
- `target-schedule.json`
- `decision-evidence.json`
- `report.md`
- `manifest.json`

JSON is canonical UTF-8 with LF endings, exact Decimal strings, stable ordering, and no clock. The
manifest records exact byte sizes and SHA-256 values. Verification checks the closed inventory,
hashes, strict contracts, per-event hashes, and then regenerates every artifact from the bundled
specification and evidence. Writes stage and validate before atomic promotion, reuse identical
output, refuse different output, and reject roots, links/reparse points, ancestor files, and other
unsafe path states.

The target schedule is the canonical existing schema v1 and needs no translation. The assembler
continues to own latency and modeled fills; accounting owns P&L; metrics own performance results;
the critic evaluates the resulting study. Intent notes retain the full baseline analytical and
source identities. Researcher-supplied schema-v1 studies without baseline metadata remain valid,
and their parse/serialize representation is unchanged.

The standalone baseline verifier proves internal bundle consistency and deterministic regeneration
from bundled specification and evidence. A self-consistent rewrite can therefore pass that
internal check. The registered `research_baseline.generate` and `research_baseline.verify` tools
add a separate origin policy. For `lagged_funding_receiver`, they require an authoritative database,
reject active SQLite journal/WAL sidecars, acquire a consistent-read barrier, hash the database,
resolve actual funding and ingestion lineage, regenerate the complete bundle, and confirm the
database bytes remain unchanged. Changed rows, duplicate/conflicting slots, predicted funding,
unsupported ingestion lineage, a stale operational snapshot, or any nonreproducible byte fails
origin attestation. Flat and static controls reject any database argument and explicitly report
`database_consulted: false`; their authority is only the versioned policy and specification.
SQLite databases configured for WAL journal mode are currently unsupported because acquiring the
barrier can itself materialize WAL/SHM sidecars; callers must checkpoint and convert them to a
sidecar-free rollback-journal snapshot before attestation.

Three identities must not be conflated:

- portable market-data identity hashes economic funding observations without local IDs or clocks;
- portable source-lineage identity hashes the recorded ingestion-run descriptors;
- operational database-snapshot identity hashes the exact SQLite main-file bytes used under the
  read barrier.

An optional typed provenance extension links an attested baseline to a historical study. When it is
present, `historical_study.run` requires the supplied closed bundle to pass internal and origin
verification, requires byte-identical canonical schedule content, and requires complete binding of
the specification, schedule, evidence, report, manifest, bundle, and attestation identities. The
typed link carries a separate SHA-256 for each of the five artifacts, including
`baseline_report_sha256`; no partial provenance or schedule substitution is accepted.

SHA-256 proves byte identity and corruption resistance, not signer authenticity or database
authorship. The current normalized schema records ingestion-run descriptors but does not link each
funding row to an immutable raw-archive object. A party able to replace the database and all
artifacts can still construct a different unsigned history; signed source provenance remains
outside this checkpoint.

## Interpretation limits

Flat is a no-exposure control. Static long and short are directional controls. The lagged funding
receiver has substantial directional price risk: receiving funding does not imply positive total
return, and historical funding persistence is not assumed. Modeled candle-open fills are not
guaranteed executable prices. Fees, spread, slippage, latency, marking, and all downstream metrics
remain supplied or calculated by the existing study layers. Short samples and incomplete coverage
limit interpretation. These baselines establish neither profitability nor live tradability.

Deferred work includes comparative multi-baseline execution, parameter selection and ranking,
broader deterministic funding/price tools, portfolios, optimization, significance tests,
walk-forward selection, execution, scheduling, additional venues, and LLM orchestration.
